About Comfort Ark Haven

We exist to provide safe, consistent and loving homes for children and young people who are unable to live with their families. Our approach is rooted in respect, relationship and hope.

Child-centred care

Every child who comes to Comfort Ark Haven is unique. We take time to listen, understand their story and build a care plan around their needs, strengths and goals.

Working together

We collaborate closely with social workers, schools, therapists and families where appropriate, so that each young person has a consistent, wrap-around support network.

Our Homes

Each Comfort Ark Haven home is designed to feel warm, welcoming and safe — more like a family home than an institution.

Haven House

A small, nurturing home for up to 3 young people, offering calm, stable surroundings and strong, trusting relationships with staff.

  • Single and shared bedrooms
  • Comfortable communal spaces
  • Local schools and amenities nearby

Ark View

A supportive home focused on building independence, confidence and life skills for older young people preparing for adulthood.

  • Personalised independence plans
  • Practical skills development
  • Encouragement with education and training

We can adapt our homes and support to meet a range of needs — please contact us to discuss.

Referral Process

We aim to make the referral process clear, timely and collaborative.

1

Initial enquiry

Local authorities and professionals can contact us by phone or email to discuss a potential placement and current availability.

2

Information sharing

We review assessments and background information to understand the young person’s needs, risks and strengths.

3

Matching & planning

We consider whether we can meet the young person’s needs within our homes and, if appropriate, develop an initial placement plan.

4

Placement & review

Once a placement is agreed, we work closely with the professional network and review progress regularly.
